# WrongSecrets Social Implementation Plan

## Purpose
Increase GitHub stars and community engagement by executing a consistent social and content strategy that drives users to try WrongSecrets and star the repository.

## Goals
- Improve visibility of WrongSecrets across social channels.
- Convert social traffic into active users and GitHub stars.
- Build a repeatable content workflow for maintainers and contributors.

## Success Metrics
- GitHub stars per month.
- Click-through rate (CTR) from social posts to repository/demo links.
- Number of social posts published per month.
- Number of new contributors and community interactions (issues/discussions).

## Audience
- Developers learning secure coding and secret management.
- Security engineers and AppSec teams.
- Students and CTF participants.
- Open source contributors.

## Execution Timeline (8 Weeks)

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Messaging and Readiness
- Define a clear social value proposition:
  - Learn secret detection through realistic challenges.
  - Practice in a CTF-style environment.
  - Start quickly with minimal setup.
- Standardize link targets:
  - Primary: GitHub repository.
  - Secondary: play/demo or quickstart documentation.
- Create reusable content templates for LinkedIn and X.

Deliverables:
- Approved message pillars.
- Post templates.
- Tracking links for campaign attribution.

### Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4): Initial Content Launch
- Select 3 high-impact existing challenges.
- Publish teaser posts per challenge:
  - 1 LinkedIn version.
  - 1 X version.
- Include clear CTA in every post:
  - Try the challenge.
  - Star the repository.

Deliverables:
- Minimum 6 published posts.
- Baseline performance report (impressions, clicks, stars).

### Phase 3 (Weeks 5-6): Campaign and Community Activation
- Run a "Challenge of the Month" campaign.
- Publish weekly recap posts with progress and highlights.
- Ask for community submissions:
  - New challenge ideas.
  - Pull requests.
  - Feedback through issues/discussions.

Deliverables:
- Monthly challenge announcement.
- Weekly recap cadence in place.
- Contributor call-to-action posts.

### Phase 4 (Weeks 7-8): Optimization and Scale
- Review performance by channel and format.
- Optimize based on highest-performing hooks and challenge types.
- Build a monthly content calendar with owners and deadlines.
- Document final process for maintainers.

Deliverables:
- Optimization report.
- Next-month content calendar.
- Maintainer playbook for recurring social execution.

## Content Pillars
- Educational: explain common secret management mistakes.
- Challenge Teasers: short puzzles with urgency and curiosity.
- Behind the Scenes: how a challenge was built and what it teaches.
- Community: celebrate contributors, PRs, and issue discussions.

## Publishing Cadence
- 2 social posts per week minimum.
- 1 weekly recap post.
- 1 monthly campaign post (Challenge of the Month).

## Roles and Ownership
- Content Owner: prepares weekly post set.
- Reviewer: validates technical accuracy and tone.
- Publisher: posts and tracks performance.
- Community Maintainer: follows up on comments and contributor questions.

## Tracking and Feedback Loop
- Use trackable links for each post.
- Measure weekly and compare against baseline.
- Capture community feedback and frequently asked questions.
- Update templates and messaging monthly.

## Risks and Mitigation
- Inconsistent posting:
  - Mitigation: use a content calendar and assign owners.
- Low engagement:
  - Mitigation: test different hooks, formats, and posting times.
- Weak conversion to stars:
  - Mitigation: strengthen CTA placement and simplify onboarding links.

## Definition of Done
- Social content pipeline is active and repeatable.
- At least 8-12 posts published during the first 8 weeks.
- Weekly performance review is established.
- Documented process exists for long-term maintenance.
